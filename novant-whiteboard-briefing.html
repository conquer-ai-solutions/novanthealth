Whiteboard Playbook

How to run this session

50 minutes. Pamela is driving. Our job is to listen more than talk. This is discovery, not a pitch.

Don't pitch. Thank Pamela for making this happen and for prioritizing this discussion. "We've been looking forward to understanding how you're thinking about AI delivery at Novant. We know you're building something thoughtful here — we'd love to hear about it." Let her lead. Make it clear we're here to learn.

These are the gaps we need to fill:

  • On the reference architecture: Walk us through the reference architecture you're building — what's the framework, what principles are guiding it? What are the key components?
  • On the AI operating model: How are you thinking about the pipeline from use case identification to delivery? Where does it flow smoothly and where does it get stuck?
  • On existing AI capabilities: You mentioned you have your own AI model — what's it doing today, and where do you see its limits? What gaps are you trying to fill?
  • On use case priorities: Of the use cases you've identified, which ones are most urgent? Which ones are hardest to deliver?
  • On vendor/partner engagement: How do you see external partners fitting into this model? Where do you need the most help?
  • On Vijay's priorities: Vijay is building out the AI function — what does his roadmap look like for the next 6-12 months?

Only if asked or natural opening: We can reference regulated industry experience (clinical trials, insurance, banks). This shows we understand compliance and governance challenges. But don't lead with it — let Pamela's conversation guide what's relevant.

Don't show: The FI demo. This isn't a pitch session. If Pamela wants to see how something might work, we can sketch it out or discuss an analogue, but this is not the time for a polished product demo.

What to draw on the whiteboard: Their process flows as we understand them. Ask Pamela to walk us through the architecture and validate our understanding visually. The corrections and details she provides are the signals we need.

This session is Discovery. The next phases would look like:

Watch-Outs

Where this can go wrong

Risks specific to Novant that can derail momentum.

Don't pitch This is NOT a sales session. Pamela organized this to share their thinking. If we come in selling, we lose the trust that got us here. We're here to listen and understand. Period.
Vijay was cautious He said "no immediate action" on the intro call. Pamela is our path in, but Vijay is the AI decision-maker. Everything we do needs to give Pamela ammunition to bring Vijay along. Make her look good.
Scale concerns may surface Vijay explicitly flagged scale as a concern. Conquer is ~30 people with an 8-person dev team. Be honest about this if asked. Frame it through delivery quality and speed rather than headcount. Healthcare is regulated — they care more about getting it right than just getting it fast.
Be honest about healthcare experience We have regulated industry experience (clinical trials, insurance, banks) but limited direct healthcare deployments. Don't overstate what we've done. The clinical trials and insurance work is relevant but not identical. Be clear about what we know and what we'd need to learn.
Don't over-promise on architecture fit They're publishing a reference architecture. Don't commit to full alignment with something we haven't seen yet. Listen, understand, and come back with a thoughtful perspective on how Conquer complements what they're building.
Pamela is your champion — protect the relationship She went out on a limb to set this up after Vijay's cautious response. Make her look good. Give her material she can take back to Onyeka and Vijay. A follow-up memo or perspective that shows we listened and understood is more valuable than a pitch deck.
Competitive landscape is crowded ~10 companies are pitching Novant similar things. We need to differentiate through understanding, not volume. Show we listened. The fact that Pamela chose to have a whiteboarding session instead of a demo pitch is significant — we got this right already.
