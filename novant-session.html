02
Probe
Go deeper on what matters. Understand the gaps in their framework.
These are deployed responsively — when the context earns them. Don't run through them like a checklist. Each one locks into what Pamela just shared.
10 min · One thread at a time · Follow the energy
Q1 — Architecture
Walk us through the reference architecture — what principles are guiding it, and what does the target state look like?
Understand their framework so we can position within it, not outside it. Specific technologies, standards, where they're still building.
Listen for: Specific tools, standards they're adopting, where they've made decisions and where they're still choosing.
Q2 — Pipeline
How are AI initiatives flowing from idea to delivery today? Where does the pipeline get stuck?
Find the operational pain points. Where do ideas get bottlenecked before they become working systems?
Listen for: Specific handoff problems, governance friction, approval delays, where bandwidth is the constraint.
Q3 — Internal model
You mentioned your own AI model — what's it handling today, and where are you hitting its limits?
Understand capability boundaries. What types of tasks is it handling, what can't it do, where does it scale?
Listen for: Specific use cases it handles well, specific classes of work it can't do, scale constraints, integration barriers.
Q4 — Use cases
Of the use cases you've identified, which ones keep coming back as most urgent?
Surface priority opportunities. What's actually needed vs. what would be nice to have.
Listen for: Specific departments, workflows, patient-facing processes. Clinical impact. Regulatory drivers.
Q5 — Partners
How do you see external partners fitting into the AI operating model? What does a good partner look like to you?
Understand evaluation criteria. What matters to Pamela and Vijay when choosing who to work with.
Listen for: Scale requirements, integration needs, governance expectations, healthcare domain experience, security rigor.
Q6 — Close anchor · most important
What would make Vijay and Onyeka say 'yes, let's go deeper with these guys'?
Understand the internal sell. What does Pamela need to champion this internally?
Listen for: What removes risk in Vijay's mind. What track record matters. What timeline is acceptable.

Agentic Layer

Their model handles prediction and classification at scale. Agentic AI handles multi-step workflows: document triage, compliance checking, clinical pathway orchestration. Different layer, complementary capabilities. We layer on top of what they've built, not beneath it.
Only deploy if: Pamela described workflow automation gaps or multi-step process limitations in their model.
Healthcare-Grade Delivery at Scale
Regulated Delivery Track Record

Clinical trials, insurance, major banks — we've navigated regulated environments. The close rule
Don't leave the call without a specific date in both diaries. "We'll be in touch" is not a next step. "Perspective by Friday the 18th, Pamela reviews with Vijay by the 25th" is a next step. If they're not ready to commit — find out why before the call ends.
The goal
Pamela says "let me set up a session with Vijay and Onyeka." If we get that, we've won the session. We don't pitch scope, price, or timeline today. We just earn the mandate to come back with something thoughtful that sits within their framework.
If they ask about other solutions
"There are a lot of vendors in this space — that's actually why the framework matters. Our question is: who can deliver outcomes within your rules, not who has the flashiest demo. That's what next steps are for — showing you how we'd actually work with what you've built."
